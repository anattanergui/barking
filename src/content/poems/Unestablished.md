# Unestablished
This camera is simultaneously capturing and displaying
A three-dimensional world of height, width and depth
Complete with sensory textures
Its lenses are eyes
Its side-attachments, ears
Its tripod, legs
It carries both microphone and speaker
Processing input, projecting output 
Each part engaging the world

Yet, this world —
Displayed on the mind’s screen
Filtered through the camera’s senses
Is entirely subjective
It can never be known to exist objectively

For it appears 
Only in wakefulness
When the camera is lit
When experience arises
As subject encountering the world

That same world, so real in waking
Vanishes in sleep
When the camera is dark —
With no subject to experience the world

The appearance of the world 
Depends upon the subject’s presence in waking
And disappears
With its absence in sleep

Even if 500 million people came together
Wrote theses to prove an objective world
Built libraries, carved words in stone
And hung banners across the cosmos declaring:
This universe is objective —
It existed before you
And will exist after you

No matter what they do
The proof of an objective existence —
Of any part of the world or universe —
Can never be found
Nothing has ever been known 
To exist apart from the subject

Nor can the subject be established
For if it were, it would be an object
And no object has ever 
Been truly known to exist

Thus, in direct experience 
Neither subject nor object
Can be known to hold
Any substantial, independent existence

The camera — the perceiver
Its recording, perception
Its display — the perceived 
Neither holds independent existence
Their arising is co-arising
And what they are
Cannot even be said to exist
