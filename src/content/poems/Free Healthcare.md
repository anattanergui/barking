# Free Healthcare
You get sick
Call emergency
An ambulance arrives
Picks you up and carries you
To a hospital 

Where you receive
Surgery, transplant, or treatment
And return home
To live another day

What a country — you think
No bills, no cost
For being processed, fixed, and housed
In a hospital
And then released
To face another day —
For free

But with dependence the cost
Your autonomy the price
As you were triaged and scheduled
Life was rationed or death administered 
Cloaked by dignity —
What did you really get
For free

And do you ever think
If you got sick
Had no phone 
And died —
Slowly or suddenly —
Where you fell:

That death came
Free —

No cost, no call, no borrowed time —
Just you, wherever you were
However you were

Is free healthcare
Or any promised safety —
Anything but sacrilege 
Against the one thing
That remains truly free

An inversion, appropriation, debasement
Of what always was
And still is free:

Turning autonomy —
Your inherent quality —
Into a quantity 
To fortify the prison
Where you survive
Your real freedom
Left outside
