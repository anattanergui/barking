# Dark Ghost Of Monday
The dark ghost of Monday 
Makes itself felt
In the chest 

Envelops the heart 
Weighs it down
Sends black wisps
Through my blood 
To my brain 
My eyes, my ears

Clouding, darkening 
My mind 

I know it lives
In me
And nowhere else 

But as what —
I want to know 
What are its components —
Atoms, molecules
Connective tissue
Bones, blood 

Can I capture it — lay it on a block 
Cut it open, and once and for all
Dispel it —
Dispatch it to hell, or wherever 
It came from 

But wouldn’t it just wander —
Like smoke, unseen 
Still there, waiting
To haunt another house, a host
Tormenting someone else —
Or come back to me, inhabit me
All over again 

Can it be uncreated —
Not just driven away
But erased
As if it never existed 

But how can a feeling 
A ghost, a wisp of smoke
A cloud —
Ever be grasped, ever dispelled

How could I, on a mountaintop —
Inside the clouds
With clouds above, below 
All around me —
And breathing them in —

Dissolve, unmake, wipe away
What I can’t see — only feel
Only sense

Wouldn’t it be like 
Trying to dispel the air
I breathe

Trying to grasp 
The light that surrounds me
To lay it on a block, cut it open
Extinguish it — eliminate it

Or like capturing the scent of a rose —
Because it envelopes, suffuses, penetrates —
My mind, my skin, my cells 

Is the ghost any different
From the clouds, or light, or scent —
Only in its density, temperature
Colour, and smell

Am I just chasing — 
Light
Pleasant scents, softer air
Appealing sights

While judging, pushing —
The dark ghost — wanting it cut, dissected
Banished to hell
As if it were evil — a demon

While I’m immersed —
In its twin — the ghost of sun
The light —
Without which I’d never feel
Never even know
The dark ghost
Of Monday
