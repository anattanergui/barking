# A Costume Speak
Disdain
And then — the slow attempt
To speak across a gap
That’s not just thought
But being

How does a ghost
Wearing a man costume
Speak to another
Who believes he is the costume

How do you say
To someone 
Who calls it skin
What’s but a drape

With talks
Of his past
Of what he did and what was done
What others said —
He weaves the fabric
And calls it
His self

He speaks and speaks
As if he would come undone
If he didn’t speak —

Pointing to patterns
Colours and stitches
He confirms his worth
Affirms the form
Of his core

Others, he sees
As giants —
Robed and cloaked —
Or pygmies
In rags and tatters
None his equal

Your mask —
Transparent 
Barely in place —
You are lost for words
And wonder what to say

You move closer
And poke a finger
At the costume
To check if there really is
Something beneath

Hitting a thick
Hard shell
You wonder if
The costume has swallowed the ghost —
Or if you’d only been hearing 
A costume speak
