# Absence Of Absence
Anyone who has slept
And then woken 
Knows first hand

In sleep, there was absence —
Of all that they, in waking, take for granted

That during sleep — they, themselves — were absent
As were their worries, pains and gains
Others, world, even the universe
Everything — even their own body —
Was absent 

But what does this absence mean

Does it not mean
That ordinary apprehension —
The whole field of waking
Was not there

During sleep
There was neither perceived
Nor perceiver

The absence does not refer
To the absence of something concrete 
Objectively present — during waking —
But rather
The absence of the absence of presence

For the perceiver — in and during waking — 
Is already an absence — a void — 
Mistaken for substantial presence 

In sleep, even this apparent presence is absent —
The absence of presence of absence

In other words —
It is not something that goes absent during sleep
But rather that
Something illusory — 
Appears present during waking 

And that which appears present
Cannot be other than that which is
Absent in sleep

Both presence and absence 
Are conceptual constructions 
For that —  called presence —  is already absence
And that — called absence 
Is the absence of apparent presence 

What actually IS —
In waking and in sleep —
Must therefore
Be beyond both — neither —
Presence and absence
