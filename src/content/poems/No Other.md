# No Other
How could one possibly
Ever 
Love another

When there is not 
Can never be 
Another

Wherever one looks 
It seems as if there are 
Others
Endless numbers of others 

But since when have
Numbers become others
Since when have 10 and 10,000 
Become others to 5 and 5,000

And if one were to love another 
A random other 
What would one do
With all the rest — the countless others

Hate them, be indifferent
Something other
What is love for another  — one other 
But unlove for some other 
For most others

Loving other cannot be done
For there is no other 
And to love one other
Is to unlove most others

And if what — is called love
Is changeable
Fashioned and conditional
In action and expression 

Love then — if it were to be
The principle 
Universal, unconditional 
Could not be changeable 

But a vision 
Of absolute equanimity
No instant ever being more
Or less 
Than another 

And if love were to be
Such a principle 
Unchangeable, unconditional 
Then it could not be called love, at all
And love, then
Must be only what is called love

And this —
But just 
A vision of love
